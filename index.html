Quick start

  1. Add your first transaction. Click the ＋ Add button on Transactions, or hit Upload Data to bring in a CSV or PDF statement.
  2. Review the preview. If you upload a statement, Ledger shows the parsed rows and a total so you can confirm it matches before importing.
  3. Categorise. Ledger auto-categorises using keyword rules. You can edit categories inline — it learns from your corrections.
  4. Set up sync (optional). Connect Google Drive to back up and sync across devices. See the sync section below.

Features at a glance

  • Dashboard — monthly, 3-month, year-to-date and full-year views with category tiles, spending pace, and trend arrows.
  • Transactions — searchable, filterable, bulk-edit. Multi-select to re-categorise or delete in one go.
  • Income — log salary, transfers from friends, or any offset against a category.
  • Savings — track multiple accounts. Balances are always derived from your contribution log (never edited directly).
  • Mortgages — amortisation schedule, voluntary payments, balance history.
  • Budget targets — set a monthly target per category; tiles show a progress bar.
  • Recurring detection — Monthly Commitments card auto-surfaces subscriptions and regulars.
  • Duplicate checker — scans for suspiciously similar transactions before they clutter your history.
  • Dark mode — toggle in the topbar. Works everywhere.

Google Drive sync

Ledger does not store your transactions. If you want data to survive browser clears or sync across devices, connect Google Drive — Ledger saves a single JSON file (ledger-data.json) into your own Drive.

How to connect (one click):

  1. Open Settings → Cloud Sync (Google Drive).
  2. Click Connect Google Drive and authorise Ledger on the Google consent screen.
  3. Use Push to save your current data to Drive, and Pull on another device to load it.

Who can see your data: Ledger only requests access to files it creates in your Drive — it cannot read the rest of your Drive. The access token never leaves your browser. If you share the file with someone else from Drive, they'll be able to see it — otherwise only you can.

FAQ

Where is my data stored?

Everything lives in your browser's localStorage. If you connect Google Drive, a copy also lives there as a JSON file in your own Drive. Ledger's creator never sees any of it.

What happens if I clear my browser or switch devices?

If you haven't connected Drive, data is lost. With Drive connected, pull from Drive on the new device and everything is restored.

My CSV or PDF import looks wrong — what do I do?

Use the preview in the import modal to spot bad rows before committing. The total at the bottom should match your statement. If it doesn't, try remapping the columns (for CSV) or switching between bank/credit statement type (for PDF).

Can I edit a category after the fact?

Yes. Click the category badge on any transaction row to change it. Ledger learns from repeated corrections of the same merchant.

Why can't I edit a savings balance directly?

Balances are always derived from your contribution log. This prevents drift. Add a contribution (positive or negative) if you need to adjust.

How do I handle shared expenses with a partner?

Log the transaction once, then log an offsetting income entry under Income → Transfer with the partner as the person and the original category as the offset. The net effect: you only see your share.

Can I export my data?

Yes. The ↓ Export button in the topbar dumps everything as CSV. The Google Drive JSON file is also a permanent export by design.

Does Ledger work offline?

Yes for all local features. Google Drive sync requires a connection.

What does "Clear demo data" do?

It wipes everything local so you can start with a blank slate. It does not touch any Google Drive file you may have connected.

Still stuck?

Email the maintainer directly. Include a screenshot of the import preview or the error message — it's almost always enough context.

The creator can never see your financial data, so specifics from you are what make support work.
